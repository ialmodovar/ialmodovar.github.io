Teaching

This a list of courses I had taught over my career

  • Applied Multivariate Statistical Analysis: Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R and Python.
  • Theory of Linear Models : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Theory of Statistics : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Applied Linear Regression : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R and Python.
  • Introduction to Statistical Learning : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R and Python.
  • Probability Theory : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Introduction to Experimental Design : Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Introduction to Applied Statistics: Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Data Mining: Full instructor, prepared lectures and evaluations. Software used R.
  • Applied Multivariate Analysis: Full instructor in charge of lectures and homework. Techniques for displaying and analyzing multivariate data. Software used R.
  • New Topics in Biostatistics: Co-teaching instructor, in charge of lectures and homework. Topics in machine learning. I led sessions on topics including time series, classification, principal components, and clustering in the health sciences, specifically for epidemiology students.
  • Computational Statistics: Full instructor in charge of lectures, homework, and exams. Introduction to the R statistical software in the health sciences.
  • Clinical Laboratory Statistics: Full instructor in charge of lectures, homework, and exams. Introductory statistics class for medical technologist majors.
  • Applied Nonparametric Statistics: Full instructor in charge of lectures, homework, and exams. Topics covered are nonparametric methods and inference in the health sciences. Software used R.
  • Applied Survey Sampling: Full instructor in charge of lectures, homework, and exams. Topics include sample surveys, survey processes, and methods of designing sample surveys in the health sciences. Software used R.
  • Introduction to Biostatistics I & II: Full instructor in charge of lectures, homework, and exams. Topics covered include statistical concepts and their applications in health science, as well as data collection, organization, and analysis, along with elementary probability, distributions, regression, estimation, and hypothesis testing. Software used R.
  • Introduction to Business Statistics: Teaching graduate assistant in charge of the laboratory section. Topics: multiple regression analysis, model diagnostics, applications in the analysis of variance and time series using JMP.
